Health Insurance Eligibility Info:



Most common eligibility requirements:


Age: Most health coverage plans in California have age as an eligibility criteria. Since older people are more likely to get sick and go to the hospital, health insurance geared toward this age group tends to cover more than health insurance geared toward a younger demographic. Usually,


Citizenship Status: Most health coverage plans look at your U.S citizenship status as an eligibility criteria. In California, health coverage such as Medi-cal and Covered California do not consider a person's citizenship status.


Income: All health coverage plans in the U.S and California ask for your yearly income. Low-income individuals have higher priority for health coverage, while people with high yearly income have lower priority.



Health coverage in California:


Medi-cal (Medicaid for California specifically): offers free or low-cost health coverage for California residents
In order to be eligible, you must be a California resident and meet necessary income requirements.
They have recently gotten rid of citizenship as a requirement


Covered California: offers income-based financial support as well as health coverage
In order to be eligible, you must be a California resident, a U.S citizen, national, or lawfully present immigrant, you must not be incarcerated,
and to get subsidies you must not be eligible for Medi-cal, Medicare, or affordable employment coverage
